### Ropes Courses, Trivia Nights, and the Elusive Art of Team Building #### Team building is not something you should leave to chance Building the right team is a delicate dance of personality assessments, trust falls, and the occasional obligatory happy hour --- all in the name of creating a cohesive, high-performing group. It's enough to make even the most extroverted among us want to hide under our desks. Now, don't get me wrong, I'm a big believer in the power of team building. After all, we're talking about the lifeblood of any successful project --- the people who make the magic happen. But the truth is, there's no one-size-fits-all approach when it comes to bringing a group of individuals together and getting them to work in harmony. Take the classic team building activities, for example. You know the ones I'm talking about --- the trust falls, the human knots, the spaghetti tower challenges. They all have one thing in common: they're designed to force people out of their comfort zones and into a shared, often absurd, experience. And the theory is that this shared suffering will somehow forge unbreakable bonds of camaraderie. But in reality, these "forced fun" activities often fall flat, leaving participants feeling more awkward and uncomfortable than anything else. I mean who wants to be the one to admit that they're just not feeling the team spirit while the rest of the group is frantically constructing a tower of marshmallows and spaghetti? Or share 2 truths and a lie with the people who are probably going to judge them --- even though judgement is out of scope during team building activities. There are the more "sophisticated" team building efforts, like [personality assessments](https://medium.com/@kylejones_47003/which-mythical-creature-are-you-personality-assessments-and-project-management-ead0c1255882). You know the ones --- they print a four-letter acronym on a ball or squishy stress ball; suddenly, you're supposed to have all the answers about your teammates' quirks and preferences. But as my colleague Eric found out, these assessments don't always hit the mark. He was convinced that his DISC profile was way off-base, until his wife promptly burst out laughing and declared, "This is you to a tee!" Turns out poor Eric was just a little too close to his own personality to see it objectively. So, if the traditional team building playbook isn't cutting it, what's a project manager to do? Well, I'd argue that the key lies in fostering genuine connections and mutual understanding, rather than forcing it through gimmicks and trust exercises. One of the most powerful team building strategies I've encountered is simply encouraging vulnerability and open communication. When people feel safe to be their authentic selves and share their unique perspectives, it paves the way for genuine collaboration and trust. For example, I had to deliver some tough news to my team about a major project delay. Instead of just rattling off the facts, I made a conscious effort to be vulnerable and share my own frustrations.

"I know this sucks," I told them, "and I'm just as disappointed as you are. But we're going to figure this out together." The response was remarkable. Rather than retreating into defensiveness or finger-pointing, my team rallied around the shared challenge and worked together to find a solution. By creating a space for open and honest dialogue, we were able to weather the storm as a united front. Of course, fostering that kind of vulnerability doesn't happen overnight. It takes time, effort, and a willingness to step outside of our comfort zones as leaders. But the payoff is immeasurable, both in terms of team cohesion and overall project success. And let's not forget the power of good, old-fashioned team bonding activities --- when done right, that is. Instead of forcing people into trust falls and human knots, try something a little more organic, like a potluck dinner or a casual happy hour. The key is to create opportunities for people to connect on a personal level, without the pressure of "team building" hanging over their heads. Maybe it's a virtual trivia night, or a group cooking class --- the specifics don't matter as much as the chance for your team to interact and get to know each other as human beings, not just co-workers. The best teams aren't just groups of people who are good at their jobs; they're groups of people who genuinely enjoy each other's company. And that kind of camaraderie doesn't come from mandatory trust exercises, but from authentic shared experiences. None of this is to say that personality assessments and other team building tools are entirely useless. In fact, when used thoughtfully and in conjunction with other strategies, they can be incredibly valuable. The key is to avoid relying on them as a crutch or a quick fix, and instead use them as a starting point for deeper conversations and mutual understanding. Take the Deloitte Business Chemistry model, for example. This assessment provides concrete, actionable insights on how to best support and motivate each unique style. Suddenly, those "Pioneering Trailblazers" and "Guardians" on your team feel a little less like mythical creatures and a little more like real, complex human beings. So, the next time you find yourself staring down the barrel of a team building session, take a deep breath and resist the urge to reach for the nearest spaghetti noodle. Instead, focus on creating opportunities for vulnerability, authenticity, and (dare I say it) a little bit of fun. Your team will thank you for it --- trust falls and all.
